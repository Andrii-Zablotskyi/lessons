Lobanovsky Valery Vasilievich

Lobanovsky Valery Vasilievich (1939-2002) – soccer player Valery Lobanovsky Of the players who have ever played in the Odessa “Chernomorets”, the loudest name is Valery Lobanovsky. Odessa period an outstanding player and coach was short-lived, but left a noticeable mark in his biography.


Valery Vasilyevich Lobanovsky was born on January 6, 1939 years in Kyiv. Early on he became interested in football, which became his profession, although Valery graduated from high school with a silver medal and (which is completely uncharacteristically for football players) entered the Kyiv Polytechnic institute.


His career as a player in the Kyiv "Dynamo" quickly went to mountain. Champion of the USSR (1961), winner of the USSR Cup (1964), involved for games in the national team and the Olympic team of the USSR. Valery Lobanovsky became famous as the inventor of a unique corner kick “dry sheet”, after which his partners often scored goals against enemy. But after the conflict with the new coach V. Maslov in 1965 V. Lobanovsky moved to the Odessa “Chernomorets”.

For two seasons spent in Odessa, V. Lobanovsky played 59 matches and scored 15 goals. However, perhaps the main result of his stay in our city began to receive a diploma of higher education in Odessa Polytechnic Institute (OPI). Oddly enough, this detail is not found reflection in numerous biographies of the outstanding football player masters, therefore we will dwell on it in more detail.


The invitation was initiated by V. Lobanovsky to Odessa was the then chairman of the Chernomorsky Basin Council DSO “Vodnik” P. Dosich. Football player with his wife and little daughter was given a two-room apartment on the street Bebel (Jewish), 42. And on April 6, 1965, by order of the rector of the OPI V. Lobanovsky was enrolled as a “5th year student of the correspondence department Faculty of Heat and Power Engineering Group PT-51z”. As they say university veterans, student V. Lobanovsky differed from many “correspondence students” serious attitude to study, as far as, of course, this was possible in conditions of constant training and moving.


However, he managed prepare and successfully defend a graduation project on the topic “Gas supply auxiliary shops of the steel-rolling plant. Dzerzhinsky". Protection took place on June 21, 1966, the student was rated “good”. By the decision of the State Attestation Commission (minutes No. 29 dated 06/21/1966) V. Lobanovsky was awarded the qualification "Industrial thermal power engineer".


Meanwhile, things in “Chernomorets” were not going well. In many ways it was due to coaching miscalculations. V. Lobanovsky, becoming team captain, tried to prove that it was necessary to change both tactics and training system. Disagreements sometimes reached the point that the football player took a few balls and trained on his own. So it's possible with to a certain degree of approximation to say that it is in “Chernomorets” by V. Lobanovsky took the first steps towards his coaching future.


However, this happened after the March 1967 the footballer left the Odessa team. Valery Lobanovsky Further the biography of V. Lobanovsky is well known. He is considered to be one of the best coaches in the history of world football. Infinitely Devoted his work, V. Lobanovsky and died on May 13, 2002 in Zaporozhye, during football match. Valery Lobanovsky His name is immortalized in the title Kyiv stadium.


Memorial plaque to the honored coach of the USSR Valery Lobanovsky installed on the facade of the Odessa National Polytechnic University, where he studied in 1965-66. And in the university archive stores a student card, a grade book and other documents of a graduate of this university - Valery Lobanovsky.
